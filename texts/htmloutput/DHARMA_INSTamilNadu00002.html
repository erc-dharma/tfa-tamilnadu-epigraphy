Translation by G. Vijayavenugopal

1Prosperity! Fortune! Twenty seventh year, ninetieth day of the king who took the head of the Pāṇḍya[...]Kantaḷūr

2Arumoḻitēvaccaruppētimaṅkalam, who graciously took the Eastern country, the Ganges and Kaṭāram

3? (vakaiyālaṉ) of the category veḷḷāṉvakai kīḻ Iṟaikkuṭalāka nilattukkut tavirntu

4so that it is put in the account, he graciously uttered of his glorious mouth the (officer in charge of) tirumantiravōlai Caḷukki-k-kulakāla

5...cēkaranallūr kiḻavar Araiyar ... yilūraṉ Maṇṇai-koṇṭa-cōḻa...

6of the Kṣatra-cikāmaṇi-vaḷanāṭu Cōḻa-mūvē...

Commentary

2 Kaṭāram is a port in Southeast Asia. The phrase “who graciously took the Eastern country, the Ganges and Kaṭāram” is formulaic and found in many cōḻa inscriptions”.
