Aracalāpuram, memorial stone for a cock

Metadata

Identifier:

INSTamilNadu00009

Summary:

Memorial stone set up in honour of a cock, which fought for the western quarters (mēl-cēri) of a village. It is not clear whether the stone commemorates the death of the cock when fighting (as for deceased warrior) or its victorious fight.

Hands:

There are characters of eccentric ductus: ku and k=ka (line2).

Translation by Emmanuel Francis and Valérie Gillet

1-3 The cock, which was scarred↓1 when fighting for the Mēṟcēri↓2 of Mukaiyūr.

Notes

↑1. translates as “was engraved” as he equates karukiya with karukkiya. We suggest that karukiya is the past form of karuku-tal.
↑2. That is, the western quarters.
